SECTION 6: MOAT MAP — COMPETITIVE DEFENSIBILITY
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6.1  EASILY COPYABLE (table stakes — not your moat)

     • The general concept of "AI predicts virality"
     • A dashboard with scores and recommendations
     • Using LLMs to analyze video transcripts
     • Basic trend monitoring from public APIs
     • Generic creator tips and suggestions
     • Surface-level marketing claims about accuracy

     STRATEGY: Do not rely on any of these for differentiation.
     These are the cost of entry, not the moat.


6.2  HARD TO COPY (meaningful differentiation)

     • Niche-specific content taxonomy for talking-head
       educational video
     • Event classification framework with typed responses
     • Creator-specific performance baselining methodology
     • Cross-niche bleed-pattern mapping
     • Outcome normalization across follower count, timing,
       niche saturation, and platform state
     • A recommendation engine that generates actionable
       briefs (not just scores)
     • Product UX that makes complexity feel simple
     • A working feedback loop from prediction to outcome
       to model refinement

     STRATEGY: Build these well. They create 12-18 months
     of lead time over a well-funded competitor. But they
     are replicable with enough effort and domain knowledge.


6.3  EXTREMELY HARD TO COPY (your real moat)

     • Years of proprietary, labeled, structured performance
       data across niches, creators, and content types
     • Private ontology mapping events, hooks, creators,
       audiences, and response patterns
     • Historical archive linking cultural events to creator
       responses and measured outcomes
     • Brief-to-performance feedback loops refined across
       thousands of recommendations
     • Derived intelligence datasets that exist only because
       your system has been running and learning
     • Creator trust and market position as the system
       that "sees the market better than anyone"
     • Internal institutional knowledge about what failed,
       what misled the system, and how you corrected it
     • The compound system: data + taxonomy + evaluation +
       workflow + trust + learning loops, all reinforcing
       each other simultaneously

     STRATEGY: This is the real moat. Every customer, every
     video, every prediction, every outcome, every correction
     makes this harder to replicate. Protect it aggressively.
     This is what lives in HoldCo.

SECTION 7: EXIT STRATEGY OPTIONS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

7.1  OPTION A: SELL OPCO, RETAIN HOLDCO (PREFERRED)

     What you sell:    Customers, contracts, revenue, team, brand
     What you keep:    Core IP, datasets, taxonomies, trade secrets
     Buyer receives:   License to use the IP (per Section 3 terms)
     Your ongoing:     License revenue from buyer + freedom to
                       license IP to other verticals or markets

     RISK: Sophisticated buyers may demand IP ownership.
     Mitigation: Make the license durable and commercially
     reasonable so buyer has operational security.

7.2  OPTION B: SELL EVERYTHING (OPCO + HOLDCO)

     What you sell:    The entire apparatus including all IP
     What you keep:    Nothing (or a consulting/advisory role)
     Buyer receives:   Full ownership of everything
     Your ongoing:     Purchase price only

     RISK: You lose all future optionality.
     When this makes sense: If the offer is large enough
     that retaining IP has lower expected value than the
     premium the buyer pays for full ownership.

7.3  OPTION C: SELL OPCO, LICENSE HOLDCO, BUILD AGAIN

     What you sell:    OpCo (same as Option A)
     What you keep:    HoldCo with full IP
     Next move:        License the same IP to a new OpCo in a
                       different vertical, geography, or market

     Example: Sell the creator-focused business. License the
     same cultural intelligence system to an agency, a media
     company, or a brand marketing platform.

     RISK: Non-compete clauses in the OpCo sale agreement
     may restrict this. Negotiate carefully.
